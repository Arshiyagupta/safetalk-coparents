SafeTalk Account Deactivation

← Back to Home

⚠️ Important: Deactivation is Permanent

Account deactivation is permanent and irreversible. Once deactivated, you cannot reactivate your account. If you wish to use SafeTalk again in the future, you must create a new account with a new email address.

How to Deactivate Your Account

To deactivate your SafeTalk account, follow these steps:

  1. Open the SafeTalk app on your device
  2. Navigate to Profile (bottom right tab)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Deactivate Account"
  4. Confirm deactivation when prompted

What Happens When You Deactivate

What Gets Deactivated:

  • Your Firebase Authentication account is permanently deleted - you cannot sign in anymore
  • Your user document is marked as deactivated in our system
  • A complete archive is created in our deleted users collection

What Gets PRESERVED (Not Deleted):

  • All messages and conversations - Every message remains in the database
  • All mediation history - AI recommendations and session records
  • All request records - With timestamps
  • All calendar events - Shared schedule remains intact
  • All expense tracking data
  • All file attachments
  • Co-parent connection - The relationship data is preserved

What Happens to Your Co-Parent

When you deactivate your account, your co-parent:

  • Receives a push notification - "Your co-parent has deactivated their account"
  • Receives an email notification explaining what happened
  • Retains full access to all shared communication history, messages, mediations, and records
  • Can still access all past conversations and download records
  • Will see you appear as "Deactivated User" in message history
  • Cannot send new messages to your deactivated account

Can You Reactivate Your Account?

No - Deactivation is permanent and irreversible. If you want to use SafeTalk again, you must create a new account with a new email address.

Why We Preserve Your Data

SafeTalk preserves communication records for important legal and practical reasons:

Legal & Court Compliance:

  • Court Admissibility - Communication records must be retained for potential court proceedings
  • Co-Parent Access - Both co-parents need access to preserved records for legal purposes
  • Evidence Protection - Prevents tampering with communication evidence
  • Court Order Compliance - Some courts require use of SafeTalk and record retention
  • Both Parties Protected - Ensures accessible communication records for both co-parents

Summary

Deactivating your account:

  • Permanently prevents you from signing in
  • Is irreversible - you cannot reactivate
  • Preserves all your communication history for legal purposes
  • Allows your co-parent to retain full access to all shared messages, mediations, and records
  • Protects both parties' need for accessible communication records
